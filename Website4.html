President Ellen Johnson Sirleaf

Ellen Johnson Sirleaf,(born October 29,1938,Monrovia,Liberia),Liberian politician and economist who was president of Liberia(2006-18).She was the first woman to be elected head of state of an African country.


Johnson Sirleaf was one of three recipients,along with Leymah Gbowee and Tawakkul Karman, of the 2011 Nobel Prize for peace for their effort to further women's rights.

Education and start of government service

Johnson Sirleaf is of mixed Gola and German heritage.Her father was the first indigenous Liberian to sit in the national legislature.She was educated at the College of West Africa in Monrovia and at age 17 married James Sirleaf(they were later divorced).In 1961 Johnson Sirleaf went to the United States to study economist and business administration.After obtaining a master's degree(1971)in public administration from Havard University,she entered government services in Liberia.


Johnson Sirleaf served as assistant minister of finance(1972-73)under President William Tolbert and as finance minister(1980-85) in Samuel K.Doe's military dictorship.She became known for her personal financal integrity and elashed with both heads of state.During Doe's regime she was imprisoned twice and narrowly election she campaigned for a seat in the Senate and openly criticized the military government, which led to her arrest and a 10-years prison sentence.She was released after a short time and allowed to leaved the country.
